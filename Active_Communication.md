## Question 1: What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

- Focus on the speaker and topic; do not get distracted by your own thoughts.
- Try not to disturb others. Let them finish and then respond.
- Use door openers. These are phrases that show you are interested.
- Show that you are listening through body language.
- Take notes during important conversations.
- Paraphrase what others have said to make sure you are on the same page.

---

## Question 2: According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

- Listen carefully to the other person and how they feel.
- Do not give advice or make any guesses.
- Show them that you understand their feelings.
- Ask if you understood them in the right way.
- Do not judge anyone based on their thoughts.
- Let them fix or explain if you got something wrong.

---

## Question 3: What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- I get lost in my own thoughts.
- I’m not able to listen to topics in which I’m not interested.
- Sometimes I show people that I’m not interested in listening to them through my body language.
- I judge people based on their thoughts.
- I get distracted by mobile phones.

---

## Question 4: What can you do to improve your listening?

- Don’t take my phone with me.
- Try not to judge anyone based on their thoughts.
- Try to show interest even if I’m not interested in a particular topic.
- Try to focus on the person who is speaking.
- Take notes of important discussions.

---

## Question 5: When do you switch to a Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- When talking to my mentors or seniors.
- When I’m in a conflict and want to avoid it.
- With people who have authority, like police, etc.
- With my friends.
- With people with whom I want to end the conversation.

---

## Question 6: When do you switch into an Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- With my siblings at home.
- In sports, especially in team games.
- In traffic when someone takes a steep cut.
- On social media when someone is bothering me.
- In high-pressure situations.

---

## Question 7: When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles?

- Mostly with my friends when we are in a good mood.
- When I work in a team.
- At home with parents.
- With relatives.

---

## Question 8: How can you make your communication assertive?

- Focus on our actual feelings as opposed to our thoughts about those feelings.
- Practice looking for the need behind the need.
- If you are shy, start practicing assertive communication with safe people and in easy situations.
- Call out behaviors that hurt us; otherwise, they tend to get worse.
